Frequently Asked Questions

This section answers common questions about competitive programming.

What's the point of Competitive Programming?

Competitive programming can help improve your algorithm knowledge and problem-solving skills. It is very useful for job interviews and to improve your thinking skills. If you get good at CPPR(Competitive Programming), you could probably ace most interviews.

When should I start?

You can start competitive programming at any time, but many people begin in high school or early college years. Starting early allows you to develop strong problem-solving skills and familiarity with common algorithms and data structures, at an early age which is quite helpful to have competitive programming. However, it's never too late to start.

What contests do YOU do?

Well, I do many contests, however, my favorites are:

  1. Any In-Person Team Contest (CodeQuest, PClassic, etc.)
  2. USACO
  3. Codeforces
